But at first, take a look at the recent statistics which illustrates the true situation in the modern world.
The statistical facts, which we’ve listed below, are really shocking. Nevertheless, this is the only way to demonstrate you how bad the situation really is.
Every 39 seconds there’s a hacking attack somewhere in the world. More than 43% of all cyber attacks targeted small business! $1,077 is the average amount demanded after a ransomware attack. 400,000 malicious programs are registered every day. If we calculate the general number, it will be 700 million of such programs to date.
7 out of 10 organizations say their security risk increased significantly in 2017. 1 in 131 emails contains a malware. 3 billion of Yahoo accounts hacked for the last few years. 40% is a year over year increase rate in mobile cyber attacks. 65% of the best US banks failed a security test. 40% of victims paid to unlock their devices after a cyber attack.

With such a cybercrime rate it’s not a surprise that more than $85 billions were spent on the information security in 2017 due to a rising danger. It was estimated that the damage of cyber crimes will have reached $6 trillion annually by 2021. If we compare this matric to 2015, we’ll see a twice less number.
Infosecurity, a trustful ten years old magazine, has even worse predictions for the future in the cybersecurity field. The source sees the deterioration in a few sections. And the first one is:
Ransomware. It is believed that after random attacks, ransomware will focus more on some targeted victims. And one of the first to suffer would be medical devices which help to sustain vital signs of a person’s life. Heart pacemaker, for instance. Under such conditions, people would have to pay not because they want to save their gadgets but to save their lives.
Breaches. The problem of data breaches will be critical in the following few years. We can make such a conclusion due to the events which took place in 2017. For instance, Equifax, an American customer credit reporting agency which collects and stores information on more than 800 million clients, reported about multiple data breaches in 2017. Moreover, many of them could be easily avoided. The breaches were the results of neglection and led to a cybersecurity catastrophe of the epic scale. As a result, we see the dramatic increase of identity thefts in the USA for the previous years.
What frightens us more is that even government can’t protect personal data of people. Last year personal data of more than 200 million American voters leaked online. The breach included personal information of nearly every registered voter in the country!
Apart from these, there were too many breaches to report about. Yet, this information shows how vulnerable all customers are and how low the cybersecurity protection level is in the majority of modern companies.
